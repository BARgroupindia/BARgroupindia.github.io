Product Overview

Reactive blue dyes are high-performance, water-soluble colorants engineered to deliver brilliant shades ranging from turquoise and bright blue to deep navy on cellulosic fibers. Designed with advanced reactive systems such as vinyl sulfone, phthalocyanine-based structures, and bifunctional chemistries, these dyes offer excellent fixation efficiency, strong tinctorial strength, and superior reproducibility across dyeing processes. They are optimized for compatibility with cotton, viscose, modal, and blended substrates, ensuring uniform shade development, controlled migration, and consistent results in both batch and continuous operations.

The product range supports diverse textile processing techniques including exhaust dyeing, cold pad batch, pad-dry-cure, printing, and digital textile applications. With features such as high exhaustion, low hydrolysis, reduced electrolyte requirement, and excellent wash and light fastness, these dyes contribute to improved process efficiency and lower environmental impact. Available in multiple forms including powder, granular, liquid, and ink formulations, they are suitable for applications across apparel, home textiles, denim, technical textiles, and high-end fashion segments, meeting global compliance standards and modern sustainability requirements.

Safety & Handling

Important Safety Information

Reactive Blue dyes, including vinyl sulfone types (RB19), phthalocyanine-based turquoise blues (RB7), bifunctional and high-performance navy systems (RB160, RB171, HF grades), as well as high-exhaustion, printing, and digital ink formulations, are water-soluble organic colorants designed to chemically bond with cellulosic fibers. While generally stable under recommended conditions, exposure to dye powders or solutions may cause skin sensitization, eye irritation, or respiratory discomfort. Proper industrial hygiene, controlled handling systems, and adherence to safety protocols are essential during storage, handling, and processing operations.

Storage Guidelines:
  • - Store in a cool, dry, and well-ventilated environment (recommended 5–35°C)
  • - Keep containers tightly sealed to prevent moisture absorption and hydrolysis
  • - Protect from direct sunlight, humidity, and excessive heat exposure
  • - Avoid contamination from incompatible chemicals and foreign materials
  • - Store away from strong oxidizing agents and highly reactive substances
Required PPE:
  • - Dust respirators (N95 / FFP2 or equivalent) during powder handling
  • - Safety goggles or chemical splash-resistant eyewear
  • - Chemical-resistant gloves (nitrile / neoprene)
  • - Protective clothing for prolonged or repeated exposure
Handling Precautions:
  • - Minimize dust generation and avoid inhalation during handling
  • - Use local exhaust ventilation (LEV) or enclosed systems where possible
  • - Prevent aerosol or mist formation in liquid dye handling
  • - Avoid direct contact with eyes and prolonged skin exposure
  • - Use wet cleaning or vacuum systems instead of dry sweeping
  • - Maintain good housekeeping to prevent dye contamination and spillage

Safety Data Sheets (SDS) are available upon request for detailed information on occupational exposure limits, regulatory compliance (REACH, ZDHC, OEKO-TEX®), and safe handling practices.

Packaging & Transportation

Reactive Blue dyes are supplied in various packaging formats depending on physical form (powder, granular, liquid, printing paste, or digital ink) and textile processing requirements. Packaging systems are designed to ensure product stability, moisture protection, and safe handling across domestic and international supply chains.

Packaging Options:
  • Paper Bags / HDPE Bags: Typically 25 kg laminated or multi-wall bags with inner liners for powder dyes
  • FIBC / Jumbo Bags: 500–1000 kg bulk packaging for large-scale textile processing
  • Fiber Drums: 25–50 kg drums for high-purity or specialty grades
  • Liquid Dyes: Supplied in HDPE drums or IBC tanks (200–1000 liters)
  • Granular Grades: Low-dust granules for improved handling and safety
  • Customized Packaging: Available as per customer specifications and export requirements
Palletization & Export Packing:
  • - Bags and drums palletized on heat-treated wooden or plastic pallets (ISPM-15 compliant)
  • - Typically 40–50 units per pallet depending on packaging configuration
  • - Stretch-wrapped and securely strapped for stability during transport
  • - Use of moisture barriers and protective liners where required
  • - Clear labeling with product name, grade, batch number, and handling instructions
Container Loading & Handling:
  • - Loaded into 20 ft / 40 ft containers as per international shipping standards
  • - Proper weight distribution maintained to prevent load shifting
  • - Use of dunnage, pallet locks, and securing materials where required
  • - Pre-dispatch inspection ensures packaging integrity and compliance
Transportation & Logistics:
  • - Generally classified as non-hazardous for transport (subject to SDS classification)
  • - Suitable for road, rail, sea, and air freight (as applicable)
  • - Handled under standard shipping conditions for packaged or bulk cargo
  • - Full documentation support including COA, SDS, and export documents

Flexible packaging, labeling, and logistics solutions are available to meet specific textile processing requirements and customer supply chain needs.
