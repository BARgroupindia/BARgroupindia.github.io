Product Overview

Pigment violet colorants comprise a versatile range of organic and inorganic chemistries engineered to deliver deep, high-chroma shades with excellent tinting strength, dispersion performance, and application flexibility. Organic variants based on dioxazine, quinacridone, carbazole, and perylene structures offer superior color purity, transparency, and high tint efficiency, making them highly suitable for inks, coatings, and plastics. These grades provide a broad performance spectrum — from cost-effective solutions for general-purpose applications to high-performance systems with outstanding lightfastness, chemical resistance, and long-term color stability. Their compatibility with various formulation systems ensures consistent processing behavior across solvent-based, water-based, and polymer-based applications.

Inorganic variants, including cobalt, manganese, and mixed metal oxide compositions, are designed for applications requiring exceptional durability, thermal stability, and resistance to harsh environmental conditions. These grades exhibit excellent opacity, UV stability, and resistance to chemicals, making them ideal for high-temperature processing, exterior coatings, and construction materials. Across the portfolio, optimized particle size distribution and surface treatment technologies enhance dispersibility, reduce processing time, and improve end-product performance. The availability of multiple physical forms — including powders, presscakes, dispersions, and masterbatches — supports efficient integration into diverse industrial systems while meeting global regulatory and sustainability requirements.

Pigment Violet 3 (PV3)

Carbazole violet pigment providing a reddish violet shade with good color strength and cost efficiency. Widely used in general-purpose coatings and inks where moderate fastness is acceptable.

  • Reddish violet shade (warm tone)
  • Moderate to high tint strength (95–110%)
  • Good dispersion & flow properties
  • Economical alternative to high-performance violets
  • Available in powder, paste & aqueous dispersion

Applications: Decorative paints, general industrial coatings, printing inks, paper coloration, rubber, textile printing.

Pigment Violet 14 (PV14)

Cobalt phosphate-based inorganic violet pigment offering excellent heat resistance, lightfastness, and chemical durability for demanding applications.

  • Bright violet shade (slightly bluish)
  • Exceptional heat stability (up to 500°C+)
  • Outstanding lightfastness & weather resistance
  • Low oil absorption & high opacity
  • Ceramic-grade and high-temperature variants available

Applications: Ceramics, glass, enamels, coil coatings, powder coatings, architectural coatings, high-temperature plastics.

Pigment Violet 15 (PV15)

Manganese ammonium pyrophosphate pigment (Manganese Violet) known for its soft violet tone, excellent stability, and suitability for cosmetic and specialty applications.

  • Soft bluish violet shade (low chroma)
  • Excellent heat & light stability
  • Good chemical resistance
  • Low oil absorption & smooth texture
  • Cosmetic-grade and micronized grades available

Applications: Cosmetics (eye shadow, face products), coatings, plastics, ceramics, artist colors, specialty formulations.

Pigment Violet 19 (PV19)

Quinacridone-based high-performance pigment delivering outstanding lightfastness, weather resistance, and color purity across demanding applications.

  • Clean violet to reddish violet shades
  • High tint strength (110–130%)
  • Excellent lightfastness (7–8 Blue Wool)
  • Superior weather & chemical resistance
  • Available in surface-treated, dispersion & masterbatch forms

Applications: Automotive OEM & refinish coatings, industrial coatings, plastics (engineering polymers), fibers, high-end inks.

Pigment Violet 23 (PV23)

Dioxazine violet pigment known for its extremely high tinting strength and deep bluish shade, making it one of the most widely used violet pigments globally.

  • Deep bluish violet (very high chroma)
  • Very high tint strength (120–150%)
  • Excellent dispersion & transparency
  • Good lightfastness & solvent resistance
  • Available in powder, presscake, flush & dispersion forms

Applications: Printing inks (offset, flexo, gravure), packaging inks, plastics, coatings, digital inks, laminates.

Pigment Violet 29 (PV29)

Perylene-based high-performance pigment offering exceptional durability, chemical resistance, and heat stability for engineering and specialty applications.

  • Deep violet to maroon shade
  • High durability & chemical resistance
  • Excellent lightfastness (8 Blue Wool)
  • High heat stability (300°C+)
  • Suitable for high-performance polymers

Applications: Engineering plastics (PEEK, PET, nylon), automotive components, high-temperature coatings, fibers, aerospace applications.

Pigment Violet 37 (PV37)

Modified dioxazine violet pigment offering improved dispersibility and processing stability compared to PV23, suitable for high-speed and advanced applications.

  • Bluish violet shade (high strength)
  • High tint strength (115–135%)
  • Improved dispersion & rheology control
  • Better process stability vs PV23
  • Available in flush, dispersion & ink concentrate forms

Applications: High-speed printing inks, UV inks, digital inks, plastics, industrial coatings, packaging inks.

Pigment Violet 39 (PV39)

Mixed metal oxide inorganic violet pigment engineered for extreme durability, weather resistance, and long-term outdoor stability.

  • Dull to medium violet shade
  • Excellent weather & UV resistance
  • High heat stability (300–500°C+)
  • Low migration & high chemical resistance
  • Infrared reflective (cool pigment grades available)

Applications: Exterior coatings, façade coatings, roofing materials, plastics for outdoor use, infrastructure coatings, powder coatings.

Safety & Handling

Important Safety Information

Pigment Violet products, including carbazole and dioxazine pigments (PV3, PV23, PV37), high-performance quinacridone and perylene pigments (PV19, PV29), and inorganic pigments such as cobalt and manganese-based compounds and mixed metal oxides (PV14, PV15, PV39), are generally non-flammable, chemically stable, and insoluble materials. However, fine powders may generate airborne dust during handling, which can cause mechanical irritation to the eyes, skin, and respiratory tract. Certain inorganic and metal-containing pigments may include regulated elements (e.g., cobalt, manganese), requiring adherence to industrial hygiene practices and regulatory compliance.

Storage Guidelines:
  • - Store in a cool, dry, and well-ventilated environment
  • - Keep containers tightly sealed to prevent moisture absorption and contamination
  • - Protect from direct sunlight and excessive heat exposure
  • - Avoid contamination from incompatible materials and foreign substances
  • - Store away from strong oxidizing agents and reactive chemicals
Required PPE:
  • - Dust respirators (N95 / P2 or equivalent) in powder handling environments
  • - Safety goggles or protective eyewear
  • - Chemical-resistant gloves and appropriate industrial workwear
  • - Protective clothing for prolonged or repeated exposure
Handling Precautions:
  • - Minimize dust generation during transfer, mixing, and processing operations
  • - Use local exhaust ventilation (LEV) or dust extraction systems where required
  • - Handle materials in closed or controlled systems for bulk operations
  • - Avoid direct contact with eyes and prolonged skin exposure
  • - Use vacuum cleaning systems instead of dry sweeping or compressed air
  • - Maintain good housekeeping practices to prevent dust accumulation

Safety Data Sheets (SDS) are available upon request for detailed information on occupational exposure limits, regulatory compliance (REACH, RoHS), and safe handling practices.
