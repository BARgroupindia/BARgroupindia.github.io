Product Overview

Pigment red colorants comprise a broad spectrum of organic and inorganic chemistries engineered to deliver vibrant shades ranging from yellowish red to bluish red and deep maroon. These materials are designed for high tinting strength, controlled particle size distribution, and excellent dispersion across diverse systems including coatings, plastics, and printing inks. Economy-grade pigments provide cost-effective coloration for general applications, while advanced chemistries offer superior lightfastness, heat stability, and resistance to solvents, chemicals, and migration. This versatility enables consistent color development, high chroma, and reliable performance across both indoor and outdoor environments.

High-performance variants are specifically developed for demanding applications such as automotive coatings, engineering plastics, and specialty inks, where durability, weather resistance, and long-term color retention are critical. Inorganic options provide exceptional opacity, UV stability, and thermal resistance, making them suitable for construction materials and high-temperature processes. With availability in multiple forms including powders, dispersions, presscake, and masterbatches, these pigments support efficient processing and compatibility with modern manufacturing systems. Compliance-focused grades, including heavy metal-free and regulated formulations, further ensure alignment with global environmental and safety standards while maintaining high color efficiency and performance.

Safety & Handling

Important Safety Information

Pigment Red products, including monoazo and lake pigments (PR57:1, PR48:1, PR53:1), high-performance quinacridone, DPP, benzimidazolone, and perylene pigments (PR122, PR202, PR254, PR176, PR179, PR149), and inorganic pigments such as iron oxides, molybdate reds, and cadmium reds (PR101, PR104, PR108), are generally non-flammable, chemically stable, and insoluble materials. However, fine powders may generate airborne dust during handling, which can cause mechanical irritation to the eyes, skin, and respiratory tract. Certain inorganic grades may contain regulated heavy metals (e.g., lead, cadmium), requiring strict adherence to industrial hygiene practices and regulatory compliance.

Storage Guidelines:
  • - Store in a cool, dry, and well-ventilated environment
  • - Keep containers tightly sealed to prevent moisture absorption and contamination
  • - Protect from direct sunlight and excessive heat exposure
  • - Avoid contamination from incompatible materials and foreign substances
  • - Store away from strong oxidizing agents and reactive chemicals
Required PPE:
  • - Dust respirators (N95 / P2 or equivalent) in powder handling environments
  • - Safety goggles or protective eyewear
  • - Chemical-resistant gloves and appropriate industrial workwear
  • - Protective clothing for prolonged or repeated exposure
Handling Precautions:
  • - Minimize dust generation during transfer, mixing, and processing operations
  • - Use local exhaust ventilation (LEV) or dust extraction systems where required
  • - Handle materials in closed or controlled systems for bulk operations
  • - Avoid direct contact with eyes and prolonged skin exposure
  • - Use vacuum cleaning systems instead of dry sweeping or compressed air
  • - Maintain good housekeeping practices to prevent dust accumulation

Safety Data Sheets (SDS) are available upon request for detailed information on occupational exposure limits, regulatory compliance (REACH, RoHS), and safe handling practices.

Packaging & Transportation

Pigment Red products are supplied in multiple packaging formats depending on physical form (powder, presscake, dispersion, granules, or masterbatch) and customer processing requirements. Packaging systems are designed to ensure product integrity, moisture protection, and safe handling across domestic and international logistics networks.

Packaging Options:
  • Paper Bags / HDPE Bags: Typically 20–25 kg multi-wall or laminated bags with inner liners for powdered pigments
  • FIBC / Jumbo Bags: 500–1000 kg bulk bags for large-scale industrial applications
  • Fiber Drums: 25–50 kg drums for specialty, micronized, or high-performance grades
  • Liquid Dispersions: Supplied in HDPE drums or IBC tanks (200–1000 liters)
  • Plastic Masterbatch: Supplied in pellet or granule form for plastics processing
  • Customized Packaging: Available as per customer specifications and export requirements
Palletization & Export Packing:
  • - Bags and drums are palletized on heat-treated wooden or plastic pallets (ISPM-15 compliant)
  • - Typically 40–50 bags per pallet depending on packaging configuration
  • - Stretch-wrapped and securely strapped for stability during transit
  • - Use of moisture barriers, liners, and protective covers where required
  • - Clear labeling with product name, grade, batch number, and handling instructions
Container Loading & Handling:
  • - Loaded into 20 ft / 40 ft containers as per international logistics standards
  • - Proper weight distribution maintained to prevent load shifting
  • - Use of dunnage materials, pallet locks, and bracing where required
  • - Pre-dispatch inspection ensures packaging integrity and compliance
Transportation & Logistics:
  • - Generally classified as non-hazardous for transport (except regulated heavy metal grades such as PR104, PR108)
  • - Suitable for road, rail, and sea freight transportation
  • - Handled as packaged or bulk cargo under standard shipping conditions
  • - Full documentation support including COA, SDS, and export documentation
  • - Batch traceability maintained through labeling and quality systems

Flexible packaging, labeling, and logistics solutions are available to meet specific industrial requirements and customer processing systems.
