Product Overview

Reactive Yellow dyes are high-performance, water-soluble colorants engineered to deliver bright, clean yellow shades with excellent consistency across a wide range of cellulosic substrates. Designed using advanced reactive chemistries, these dyes form strong covalent bonds with fibers such as cotton, viscose, linen, and blends, ensuring superior wash durability and color retention. The range includes optimized systems for balanced exhaustion and leveling, high fixation efficiency, and controlled reactivity, enabling compatibility with exhaust dyeing, continuous processing, and modern low-liquor ratio technologies. Variants are also tailored for printing applications and precision digital ink formulations, offering excellent solubility, filtration stability, and sharp color definition.

This portfolio is developed to meet the evolving needs of textile manufacturers, combining high brightness, reproducibility, and process efficiency with environmental responsibility. Enhanced dye utilization, low hydrolysis characteristics, and reduced electrolyte requirements contribute to lower effluent load and improved sustainability performance. The products are suitable for diverse applications including apparel, home textiles, industrial fabrics, and high-value fashion segments, supporting both bulk production and customized design requirements. With compliance to global standards and adaptability across multiple dyeing techniques, these dyes provide a reliable and scalable solution for consistent, high-quality yellow shades in modern textile processing.

Safety & Handling

Important Safety Information

Reactive Yellow dyes, including standard vinyl sulfone grades (RY84, RY86), bifunctional high-fixation systems (RY145, HF/HFN), high-exhaustion grades (EX), printing grades (P-4G, P-5G), CPB grades, and digital ink formulations, are water-soluble reactive colorants that form covalent bonds with cellulosic fibers. While stable under recommended conditions, exposure to dye powders or concentrated solutions may cause skin irritation, eye irritation, or respiratory discomfort. Some reactive dyes may also exhibit skin sensitization potential in susceptible individuals. Proper industrial hygiene, controlled handling systems, and adherence to safety protocols are essential during storage, handling, and textile processing.

Storage Guidelines:
  • - Store in a cool, dry, and well-ventilated environment (recommended 5–35°C)
  • - Keep containers tightly sealed to prevent moisture absorption and hydrolysis
  • - Protect from direct sunlight, humidity, and excessive heat exposure
  • - Avoid contamination from incompatible chemicals and foreign materials
  • - Store away from strong oxidizing agents and highly reactive substances
Required PPE:
  • - Dust respirators (N95 / FFP2 or equivalent) during powder handling
  • - Safety goggles or chemical splash-resistant eyewear
  • - Chemical-resistant gloves (nitrile / neoprene)
  • - Protective clothing for prolonged or repeated exposure
Handling Precautions:
  • - Minimize dust generation and avoid inhalation during handling
  • - Use local exhaust ventilation (LEV) or enclosed systems where possible
  • - Prevent aerosol or mist formation in liquid dye handling
  • - Avoid direct contact with eyes and prolonged skin exposure
  • - Use wet cleaning or vacuum systems instead of dry sweeping
  • - Maintain good housekeeping to prevent dye contamination and spillage

Safety Data Sheets (SDS) are available upon request for detailed information on occupational exposure limits, regulatory compliance (REACH, ZDHC, OEKO-TEX®), and safe handling practices.

Packaging & Transportation

Reactive Yellow dyes are supplied in multiple packaging formats depending on product form (powder, granular, liquid, printing paste, or digital ink) and textile processing requirements. Packaging systems are designed to ensure product stability, moisture protection, and safe handling across domestic and international supply chains.

Packaging Options:
  • Paper Bags / HDPE Bags: Typically 25 kg laminated or multi-wall bags with inner liners for powder dyes
  • FIBC / Jumbo Bags: 500–1000 kg bulk packaging for large-scale textile processing
  • Fiber Drums: 25–50 kg drums for high-purity, printing, or specialty grades
  • Liquid Dyes: Supplied in HDPE drums or IBC tanks (200–1000 liters)
  • Granular Grades: Low-dust granules for improved handling and reduced airborne particles
  • Digital Ink & Printing Forms: Supplied in sealed containers or cartridges for precision applications
  • Customized Packaging: Available as per customer specifications and export requirements
Palletization & Export Packing:
  • - Bags and drums palletized on heat-treated wooden or plastic pallets (ISPM-15 compliant)
  • - Typically 40–50 units per pallet depending on packaging configuration
  • - Stretch-wrapped and securely strapped for stability during transport
  • - Use of moisture barriers and protective liners where required
  • - Clear labeling with product name, grade, batch number, and handling instructions
Container Loading & Handling:
  • - Loaded into 20 ft / 40 ft containers as per international shipping standards
  • - Proper weight distribution maintained to prevent load shifting
  • - Use of dunnage, pallet locks, and securing materials where required
  • - Pre-dispatch inspection ensures packaging integrity and compliance
Transportation & Logistics:
  • - Generally classified as non-hazardous for transport (subject to SDS classification)
  • - Suitable for road, rail, sea, and air freight (as applicable)
  • - Handled under standard shipping conditions for packaged or bulk cargo
  • - Full documentation support including COA, SDS, and export documents
  • - Batch traceability maintained through labeling and quality systems

Flexible packaging, labeling, and logistics solutions are available to meet specific textile processing requirements and customer supply chain needs.
