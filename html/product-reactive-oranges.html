Product Overview

Reactive Orange dyes are high-performance, water-soluble colorants engineered to deliver bright, vibrant orange shades with excellent brilliance, consistency, and reproducibility on cellulosic fibers. Designed with advanced reactive chemistry systems, these dyes offer a balanced combination of high exhaustion, strong fixation, and controlled hydrolysis, ensuring efficient dye uptake and minimal wastage. The portfolio includes solutions optimized for exhaust dyeing, cold pad batch, continuous processing, and textile printing, enabling seamless integration across diverse manufacturing setups. With excellent compatibility in trichromatic systems and digital applications, they support precise shade matching and uniform color development across batches.

These dyes are developed to meet the evolving demands of modern textile processing, offering superior wash, rubbing, and light fastness along with high build-up for both medium and deep shades. Available in multiple physical forms including powder, granular, liquid, and digital ink formulations, they are tailored for operational flexibility, reduced dust handling, and improved process efficiency. The range also supports sustainable manufacturing through high fixation efficiency, low electrolyte requirements, and reduced effluent load, while complying with global environmental and regulatory standards. This makes them an ideal choice for applications spanning apparel, home textiles, fashion printing, and high-value export-oriented textile production.

Safety & Handling

Important Safety Information

Reactive Orange dyes, including standard mono-functional grades (RO4, RO7, RO16), bifunctional high-fixation systems (RO24, HF/HFN), high-exhaustion grades (EXF), printing and CPB grades, as well as digital ink formulations, are water-soluble reactive colorants designed to form covalent bonds with cellulosic fibers. While generally stable under recommended conditions, exposure to dye powders or concentrated solutions may cause skin irritation, eye irritation, or respiratory discomfort. Proper industrial hygiene, controlled handling systems, and adherence to safety protocols are essential during storage, handling, and textile processing operations.

Storage Guidelines:
  • - Store in a cool, dry, and well-ventilated environment (recommended 5–35°C)
  • - Keep containers tightly sealed to prevent moisture absorption and hydrolysis
  • - Protect from direct sunlight, humidity, and excessive heat exposure
  • - Avoid contamination from incompatible chemicals and foreign materials
  • - Store away from strong oxidizing agents and highly reactive substances
Required PPE:
  • - Dust respirators (N95 / FFP2 or equivalent) during powder handling
  • - Safety goggles or chemical splash-resistant eyewear
  • - Chemical-resistant gloves (nitrile / neoprene)
  • - Protective clothing for prolonged or repeated exposure
Handling Precautions:
  • - Minimize dust generation and avoid inhalation during handling
  • - Use local exhaust ventilation (LEV) or enclosed systems where possible
  • - Prevent aerosol or mist formation in liquid dye handling
  • - Avoid direct contact with eyes and prolonged skin exposure
  • - Use wet cleaning or vacuum systems instead of dry sweeping
  • - Maintain good housekeeping to prevent dye contamination and spillage

Safety Data Sheets (SDS) are available upon request for detailed information on occupational exposure limits, regulatory compliance (REACH, ZDHC, OEKO-TEX®), and safe handling practices.

Packaging & Transportation

Reactive Orange dyes are supplied in multiple packaging formats depending on product form (powder, granular, liquid, printing paste, or digital ink) and textile processing requirements. Packaging systems are designed to ensure product stability, moisture protection, and safe handling across domestic and international supply chains.

Packaging Options:
  • Paper Bags / HDPE Bags: Typically 25 kg laminated or multi-wall bags with inner liners for powder dyes
  • FIBC / Jumbo Bags: 500–1000 kg bulk packaging for large-scale textile processing
  • Fiber Drums: 25–50 kg drums for high-purity, printing, or specialty grades
  • Liquid Dyes: Supplied in HDPE drums or IBC tanks (200–1000 liters)
  • Granular Grades: Low-dust granules for improved handling and reduced airborne particles
  • Digital Ink & Printing Forms: Supplied in sealed containers or cartridges for precision applications
  • Customized Packaging: Available as per customer specifications and export requirements
Palletization & Export Packing:
  • - Bags and drums palletized on heat-treated wooden or plastic pallets (ISPM-15 compliant)
  • - Typically 40–50 units per pallet depending on packaging configuration
  • - Stretch-wrapped and securely strapped for stability during transport
  • - Use of moisture barriers and protective liners where required
  • - Clear labeling with product name, grade, batch number, and handling instructions
Container Loading & Handling:
  • - Loaded into 20 ft / 40 ft containers as per international shipping standards
  • - Proper weight distribution maintained to prevent load shifting
  • - Use of dunnage, pallet locks, and securing materials where required
  • - Pre-dispatch inspection ensures packaging integrity and compliance
Transportation & Logistics:
  • - Generally classified as non-hazardous for transport (subject to SDS classification)
  • - Suitable for road, rail, sea, and air freight (as applicable)
  • - Handled under standard shipping conditions for packaged or bulk cargo

  • - Compatible with modern low-liquor ratio, CPB, and energy-efficient dyeing technologies
  • - High reproducibility minimizes reprocessing, saving water, energy, and raw materials
