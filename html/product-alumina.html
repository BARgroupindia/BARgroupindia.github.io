Product Overview

Alumina (Al₂O₃) is a versatile inorganic material widely used across metallurgical, refractory, ceramic, chemical, and advanced technology industries due to its high purity, hardness, thermal stability, and chemical inertness. It is engineered to deliver consistent performance in applications ranging from metal production and high-temperature processing to precision ceramics, abrasives, and surface finishing.

With controlled chemical composition, particle size distribution, and physical properties, alumina also plays a critical role in adsorption, filtration, and catalytic processes, as well as in flame retardant systems and advanced applications such as electronics and energy storage. Manufactured under stringent quality standards using ASTM, ISO, and validated in-house methods, it ensures reliability, consistency, and suitability for diverse industrial requirements.

Metallurgical Alumina
(MA)

High-purity alumina produced via the Bayer process, primarily used as feedstock for aluminium production. It offers controlled particle size, good flowability, and consistent chemical composition for efficient smelting operations.

  • Coarse Calcined Alumina (typical PSD: 45–150 µm for optimal flow and dissolution)
  • Medium Fraction Alumina (improves packing density and electrolysis efficiency)
  • Low Soda Variants (Na₂O ~0.3–0.5% for controlled reactivity)

Key Benefits: High purity, excellent flow characteristics, stable reduction efficiency in Hall–Héroult process.

Smelter Grade Alumina
(SGA)

Refined alumina optimized specifically for aluminium smelters, ensuring consistent dissolution behavior and minimal impurities for high current efficiency.

  • Fine Powder Alumina (typical PSD: 30–100 µm for faster dissolution in cryolite bath)
  • Low Soda Alumina (Na₂O ≤ 0.1% for reduced electrolyte contamination)
  • Controlled Surface Area Alumina (enhanced reactivity and feeding performance)

Key Benefits: High dissolution rate, low impurity levels, improved smelting efficiency.

Refractory Alumina
(RA)

High-performance alumina materials designed for refractory applications offer excellent heat resistance, chemical stability, and mechanical durability. Carefully controlled particle size distribution, purity, crystal structure, and surface characteristics ensure consistent quality across grades.

  • Coarse Alumina (unground grades for high-alumina cement and aggregates)
  • Ground Alumina (3–5 microns for castables, bricks, and linings)
  • Ground Alumina with Wider Distribution (ramming masses, continuous casting refractories)
  • High Crystalline Alumina (improved thermal stability, low soda)
  • Reactive Alumina (1–3 µm for self-flow castables and shotcrete systems)
  • Sub-Micron Alumina (less than 1 µm for high-performance ceramics and ultra-dense castables)

Key Benefits: Superior high-temperature resistance, tailored PSD, and strong chemical stability.

Tabular Alumina
(TA)

Sintered alpha-alumina with high density and low porosity, produced at very high temperatures for superior thermal and mechanical performance in refractories.

  • Coarse Tabular Grains (1–8 mm for refractory aggregates)
  • Medium Grit (0.1–1 mm for castables and bricks)
  • Fine Powder (less than 45 µm for matrix bonding and densification)

Key Benefits: High bulk density, excellent thermal shock resistance, low water absorption.

White Fused Alumina
(WFA)

Electrically fused high-purity alumina with angular particles, widely used in abrasive and refractory applications due to its hardness and purity.

  • Coarse Grit (0.5–5 mm for blasting and refractory mixes)
  • FEPA Grit Sizes (F12–F220 for abrasives)
  • Micro Powder (1–10 µm for polishing and precision grinding)

Key Benefits: High hardness, chemical stability, sharp cutting edges.

Ceramic Alumina
(CA)

Fine, high-purity alumina powders engineered for advanced ceramic applications requiring high density and electrical insulation.

  • Fine Powder (0.5–5 µm for ceramic forming)
  • Spray-Dried Granules (50–150 µm for pressing applications)
  • Submicron Grades (less than 1 µm for high-density sintering)

Key Benefits: High dielectric strength, excellent wear resistance, controlled sintering behavior.

Polishing Alumina
(PA)

Ultra-fine alumina powders designed for precision polishing and surface finishing of metals, glass, and semiconductor materials.

  • Ultrafine Powder (0.05–1 µm for mirror finishing)
  • Nano Alumina (less than 100 nm for semiconductor polishing)
  • Micron Grades (1–3 µm for general polishing applications)

Key Benefits: High surface finish quality, controlled abrasion, minimal scratching.

Aluminum Trihydrate
(ATH)

Hydrated alumina used as a filler and flame retardant, releasing water upon heating to suppress flames and reduce smoke.

  • Fine Powder (1–20 µm for plastics and coatings)
  • Coarse Grades (20–100 µm for fillers)
  • Surface-Treated ATH (improved dispersion in polymers)

Key Benefits: Flame retardancy, low smoke emission, cost-effective filler.

Flame Retardant Alumina
(FR)

Specially processed alumina (typically ATH-based) used in fire-resistant formulations for cables, coatings, and polymers.

  • Fine Grades (1–10 µm for coatings and cables)
  • Ultra-Fine Grades (less than 5 µm for high-performance compounds)
  • Coated Grades (enhanced compatibility with polymers)

Key Benefits: Halogen-free flame resistance, thermal stability, environmental safety.

High Purity Alumina
(HPA)

Ultra-high purity alumina (4N–5N) used in advanced electronic and optical applications requiring minimal impurities.

  • Submicron Powder (0.1–1 µm for LEDs and semiconductors)
  • Nano Powder (less than 100 nm for advanced coatings)
  • Tablet/Bead Form (for sapphire crystal growth)

Key Benefits: Extremely low impurity levels, high optical clarity, superior electrical insulation.

Battery Grade Alumina
(BA)

High-purity alumina tailored for lithium-ion battery applications, especially separator coatings for thermal stability and safety.

  • Ultrafine Powder (0.3–1 µm for separator coating)
  • Nano Grades (less than 200 nm for high-performance batteries)
  • High Surface Area Alumina (improves adhesion and coating uniformity)

Key Benefits: Enhanced battery safety, thermal resistance, improved cycle life.

Activated Alumina
(AA)

Porous alumina with high surface area, used as an adsorbent and desiccant in gas and liquid purification systems.

  • Spherical Beads (1–8 mm for adsorption columns)
  • High Surface Area Grades (greater than 250 m²/g)
  • Granular Forms (for industrial drying systems)

Applications: Excellent adsorption capacity, moisture removal, regenerability.

Water Treatment Alumina
(AA-WT)

Activated alumina optimized for water purification, specifically for removal of fluoride, arsenic, and other contaminants.

  • Beads (2–5 mm for filtration beds)
  • High Porosity Grades (enhanced contaminant adsorption)
  • Regenerable Media (for repeated use in treatment systems)

Applications: Effective contaminant removal, safe drinking water treatment, long service life.

Safety & Handling

Important Safety Information

Alumina (Al₂O₃) is a non-flammable, chemically stable inorganic material. However, fine particles may generate dust which can cause mechanical irritation to eyes, skin, and respiratory tract. Proper industrial hygiene practices should be followed during handling.

Storage Guidelines:
  • - Store in a cool, dry, and well-ventilated area
  • - Keep containers tightly closed to prevent moisture absorption
  • - Protect from contamination and physical damage
  • - Keep away from strong acids and reactive chemicals
Required PPE:
  • - Safety goggles or dust-resistant eye protection
  • - Dust mask / respirator (N95 or equivalent) in dusty environments
  • - Protective gloves
  • - Protective clothing for prolonged exposure
Handling Precautions:
  • - Avoid generation and inhalation of dust
  • - Ensure adequate ventilation in processing areas
  • - Use dust extraction or local exhaust systems where required
  • - Avoid contact with eyes and prolonged skin exposure
  • - Handle using appropriate material handling equipment to prevent spillage

Packaging Details:

Standard packaging options include multi-layer HDPE woven bags with inner liners for enhanced moisture resistance, as well as jumbo bags (FIBC) designed for bulk handling and efficient logistics.

  • - 500 kg / 1000 kg jumbo bags (FIBC) for bulk shipments
  • - Double-lined and UV-stabilized bags for extended outdoor storage
  • - Customized packaging and private labeling available as per client requirements
Palletization & Export Handling:

For export shipments, all bags are securely palletized to ensure stability and ease of handling during loading, unloading, and transportation. Pallets are stretch-wrapped and/or shrink-wrapped with protective covering to prevent ingress of moisture and contamination.

  • - Wooden pallets (fumigated as per ISPM-15 export standards)
  • - Standard pallet configuration: 1 MT – 1.5 MT per pallet (depending on packaging type)
  • - Shrink-wrapping / stretch-wrapping with edge protection
  • - Strapping for load stability during transit
  • - Pallet covers or PE sheets for additional weather protection
Transportation & Logistics:

The material is transported under controlled conditions to maintain product integrity from dispatch to delivery. Shipments are handled through reliable logistics partners, ensuring safe and timely delivery across domestic and international markets.

  • - Containerized shipping (20 ft / 40 ft containers) for export consignments
  • - Proper container lining or stuffing to prevent moisture ingress
  • - Bulk shipments loaded using forklifts or cranes for safe handling
  • - Road, rail, and sea freight options available
  • - Documentation support including COA, MSDS, and export compliance documents

Packaging and logistics can be customized based on product grade, shipment volume, and destination requirements.

Recycling & Sustainability

Our alumina product portfolio is aligned with modern sustainability practices, focusing on resource efficiency, energy optimization, and responsible lifecycle management across refining, processing, and end-use applications.

  • - Alumina is produced primarily via the Bayer process, enabling efficient extraction from bauxite with continuous improvements in energy consumption, caustic recovery, and process optimization
  • - Closed-loop refining systems allow recovery and reuse of sodium hydroxide and process water, minimizing waste generation and reducing environmental impact
  • - High-purity and controlled PSD alumina grades improve efficiency in downstream applications (refractories, ceramics, batteries), reducing material consumption and extending service life
  • - Utilization of alumina in high-temperature refractories enhances furnace efficiency, lowering fuel consumption and associated CO₂ emissions in industries such as steel and cement
  • - Advanced calcination and sintering technologies optimize energy usage while ensuring consistent phase transformation (γ → α alumina) and product performance
  • - Recycling and reuse of alumina-containing materials (spent refractories, abrasives) contribute to circular material flows and reduced raw material dependency
  • - Activated alumina supports environmental applications such as air drying, gas purification, and removal of contaminants (fluoride, arsenic) from water systems
  • - Bulk handling systems (silos, pneumatic conveying, bulk tankers) minimize packaging waste, dust emissions, and handling losses during transportation and storage
  • - Compliance with global environmental, health, and safety standards ensures responsible use across applications including metallurgy, electronics, water treatment, and advanced materials
