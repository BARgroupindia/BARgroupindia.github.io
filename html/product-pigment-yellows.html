Product Overview

Pigment Yellow colorants represent a comprehensive range of organic and inorganic chemistries engineered to deliver consistent color performance across diverse industrial applications. The portfolio spans cost-efficient azo-based systems offering high tinting strength and brightness for printing inks and general coatings, to advanced high-performance chemistries providing superior lightfastness, weather resistance, and thermal stability. These pigments are manufactured with controlled particle size distribution and optimized surface treatment to ensure excellent dispersion, shade consistency, and compatibility across solvent-based, water-based, and polymer systems. Variations in opacity, transparency, and chroma enable precise formulation flexibility, supporting both mass coloration and high-end aesthetic applications.

Designed to meet the evolving requirements of modern industries, these pigments deliver reliable performance in plastics, coatings, inks, construction materials, and specialty applications. High-durability grades offer excellent resistance to heat, migration, and environmental exposure, making them suitable for demanding applications such as automotive coatings, engineering plastics, and outdoor systems, while inorganic variants provide exceptional opacity, UV stability, and long-term durability in harsh conditions. The range also includes eco-conscious and regulatory-compliant solutions aligned with global standards, enabling safe use in sensitive applications. With a balance of performance, efficiency, and sustainability, Pigment Yellow products serve as a versatile and dependable solution across the value chain.

Safety & Handling

Important Safety Information

Pigment Yellow products, including monoazo and diarylide pigments (PY12, PY13, PY14), high-performance organic pigments such as benzimidazolone, isoindolinone, quinophthalone, and nickel complex pigments (PY154, PY155, PY110, PY138, PY93), and inorganic pigments such as iron oxide, nickel titanate, and bismuth vanadate (PY42, PY53, PY184), are generally non-flammable, chemically stable, and insoluble materials. However, fine powders may generate airborne dust during handling, which can cause mechanical irritation to the eyes, skin, and respiratory tract. Certain inorganic and metal-complex pigments may contain regulated elements (e.g., nickel, bismuth), requiring adherence to industrial hygiene practices and regulatory compliance.

Storage Guidelines:
  • - Store in a cool, dry, and well-ventilated environment
  • - Keep containers tightly sealed to prevent moisture absorption and contamination
  • - Protect from direct sunlight and excessive heat exposure
  • - Avoid contamination from incompatible materials and foreign substances
  • - Store away from strong oxidizing agents and reactive chemicals
Required PPE:
  • - Dust respirators (N95 / P2 or equivalent) in powder handling environments
  • - Safety goggles or protective eyewear
  • - Chemical-resistant gloves and appropriate industrial workwear
  • - Protective clothing for prolonged or repeated exposure
Handling Precautions:
  • - Minimize dust generation during transfer, mixing, and processing operations
  • - Use local exhaust ventilation (LEV) or dust extraction systems where required
  • - Handle materials in closed or controlled systems for bulk operations
  • - Avoid direct contact with eyes and prolonged skin exposure
  • - Use vacuum cleaning systems instead of dry sweeping or compressed air
  • - Maintain good housekeeping practices to prevent dust accumulation

Safety Data Sheets (SDS) are available upon request for detailed information on occupational exposure limits, regulatory compliance (REACH, RoHS), and safe handling practices.

Packaging systems are designed to ensure product integrity, moisture protection, and safe handling across domestic and international logistics networks.
