Product Overview

Pigment orange colorants comprise a diverse range of organic and inorganic chemistries engineered to deliver vibrant shades from yellowish orange to deep reddish tones, with performance tailored across cost-effective and high-performance segments. These pigments exhibit excellent tinting strength, controlled particle size distribution, and consistent dispersion behavior across solvent-based, water-based, and polymer systems. Advanced organic variants provide enhanced lightfastness, weather resistance, and thermal stability suitable for demanding applications, while inorganic compositions offer superior opacity, UV resistance, and durability under extreme environmental and processing conditions. The portfolio supports both transparent and opaque systems, enabling precise color development across coatings, plastics, inks, and specialty formulations.

Designed for versatility and industrial reliability, these pigments are available in multiple forms including powders, presscakes, flushes, dispersions, granules, and masterbatches to ensure compatibility with diverse processing technologies. Their balanced performance in terms of heat resistance, migration control, chemical stability, and color consistency makes them suitable for a wide spectrum of applications such as automotive and industrial coatings, high-performance plastics, printing inks, construction materials, and consumer goods. With options ranging from economical grades for large-volume use to premium grades for high-durability applications, the range supports efficient material utilization, regulatory compliance, and long-term product performance across global industries.

Pigment Orange 5 (PO5)

β-Naphthol monoazo orange pigment providing a yellowish orange shade with good color strength and cost efficiency. Commonly used in general-purpose applications where moderate fastness is acceptable.

  • Yellowish orange shade (economy grade)
  • Moderate tint strength (95–115%)
  • Good initial brightness but limited weatherfastness
  • Easy dispersion in aqueous and solvent systems
  • Available in powder, presscake & dispersion forms

Applications: Packaging inks, paper coatings, decorative paints, rubber, low-cost coatings, stationery inks.

Pigment Orange 13 (PO13)

Diarylide orange pigment known for its strong color strength and bright shade, widely used in printing inks and coatings.

  • Bright orange with high tint strength (100–120%)
  • Good flow and printability in ink systems
  • Moderate lightfastness and solvent resistance
  • Cost-effective for large-volume applications
  • Available in powder, presscake, flush & dispersion

Applications: Printing inks (offset, flexo, gravure), packaging, laminates, decorative coatings, paper.

Pigment Orange 16 (PO16)

Diarylide-based orange pigment offering a slightly yellowish tone with good dispersion and processing properties for general coatings.

  • Yellowish orange shade
  • Moderate tint strength and brightness
  • Good dispersion in coatings and inks
  • Balanced performance vs cost
  • Available in powder and dispersion

Applications: General industrial coatings, wood coatings, construction chemicals, inks, rubber.

Pigment Orange 36 (PO36)

Benzimidazolone orange pigment delivering high heat stability and improved durability compared to diarylide pigments.

  • Clean orange shade with good chroma

  • Good migration resistance in plastics
  • Available in powder, dispersion & masterbatch

Applications: Plastics (PE, PP, PVC), injection molding, industrial coatings, packaging inks, consumer goods.

Pigment Orange 64 (PO64)

  • Available in powder, dispersion, granules & masterbatch

Pigment Orange 34 (PO34)

  • Available in powder, flush & dispersion

  • Available in powder, dispersion & masterbatch

Cadmium-based inorganic orange pigment offering exceptional heat resistance and color stability under extreme conditions.

Safety & Handling

Important Safety Information

Pigment Orange products, including monoazo and diarylide pigments (PO5, PO13, PO16), high-performance benzimidazolone and DPP pigments (PO36, PO64, PO71–74), and inorganic pigments such as iron oxides, cadmium, and molybdate oranges (PO42, PO20), are generally non-flammable, chemically stable, and insoluble materials. However, fine powders may generate airborne dust during handling, which can cause mild mechanical irritation to the eyes, skin, and respiratory tract. Certain inorganic grades may contain regulated heavy metals (e.g., lead or cadmium), and appropriate industrial hygiene and regulatory practices should be followed.

Storage Guidelines:
  • - Store in a cool, dry, and well-ventilated environment
  • - Keep containers tightly sealed to prevent moisture absorption and contamination
  • - Protect from direct sunlight and excessive heat exposure
  • - Avoid contamination from incompatible materials and foreign substances
  • - Store away from strong oxidizing agents and reactive chemicals
Required PPE:
  • - Dust respirators (N95 / P2 or equivalent) in powder handling environments
  • - Safety goggles or protective eyewear
  • - Chemical-resistant gloves and appropriate industrial workwear
  • - Protective clothing for prolonged or repeated exposure
Handling Precautions:
  • - Minimize dust generation during transfer, mixing, and processing operations
  • - Use local exhaust ventilation (LEV) or dust extraction systems where required
  • - Handle materials in closed or controlled systems for bulk operations
  • - Avoid direct contact with eyes and prolonged skin exposure
  • - Use vacuum cleaning systems instead of dry sweeping or compressed air
  • - Maintain good housekeeping practices to prevent dust accumulation

Safety Data Sheets (SDS) are available upon request for detailed information on occupational exposure limits, regulatory compliance (REACH, RoHS), and safe handling practices.

Pigment Orange products are supplied in multiple packaging formats depending on physical form (powder, presscake, dispersion, granules, or masterbatch) and customer processing requirements. Packaging systems are designed to ensure product integrity, moisture protection, and safe handling across domestic and international logistics networks.

Packaging Options:
  • Paper Bags / HDPE Bags: Typically 20–25 kg multi-wall or laminated bags with inner liners for powdered pigments
  • FIBC / Jumbo Bags: 500–1000 kg bulk bags for large-scale industrial applications
  • Fiber Drums: 25–50 kg drums for specialty, micronized, or high-performance grades
  • Liquid Dispersions: Supplied in HDPE drums or IBC tanks (200–1000 liters)
  • Plastic Masterbatch: Supplied in pellet or granule form for plastics processing
  • Customized Packaging: Available as per customer specifications and export requirements
Palletization & Export Packing:
  • - Bags and drums are palletized on heat-treated wooden or plastic pallets (ISPM-15 compliant)
  • - Typically 40–50 bags per pallet depending on packaging configuration
  • - Stretch-wrapped and securely strapped for stability during transit
  • - Use of moisture barriers, liners, and protective covers where required
  • - Clear labeling with product name, grade, batch number, and handling instructions
Container Loading & Handling:

  • - Pre-dispatch inspection ensures packaging integrity and compliance
Transportation & Logistics:

  • - Suitable for road, rail, and sea freight transportation
  • - Handled as packaged or bulk cargo under standard shipping conditions
