Product Overview

A highly versatile and essential inorganic alkali, this product is manufactured across a broad spectrum of grades to meet diverse industrial, commercial, and specialty requirements. Standard technical and industrial variants (96–99%) are widely utilized in applications such as pulp and paper processing, textiles, detergents, and water treatment. Advanced production routes, including membrane cell technology, ensure low salt content and superior purity, while diaphragm-based variants provide cost-effective solutions for less sensitive applications. Specialized forms tailored for the rayon and textile sectors offer controlled levels of iron and chloride, ensuring consistent performance in fiber production and processing environments.

For more demanding applications, high-purity and specialty grades are available to comply with stringent regulatory and quality standards. Food-grade variants conform to international specifications such as FCC and FDA, making them suitable for food processing and related industries. Pharmaceutical grades, adhering to USP and EP standards, deliver exceptional purity (up to 99.99%) for critical formulations and medical applications. Laboratory and reagent grades (AR quality) are designed for analytical and research purposes, offering precise composition and reliability. This comprehensive grade portfolio ensures adaptability across sectors, delivering consistent quality, performance, and compliance for both routine and high-precision uses.

Safety & Handling

Important Safety Information

Sodium hydroxide (NaOH), commonly known as caustic soda, is a highly corrosive and strongly alkaline compound extensively used in chemicals, textiles, pulp & paper, detergents, and water treatment industries. Direct contact with solid or concentrated solutions can cause severe burns to skin, eyes, and mucous membranes. Exposure to mists or dust may result in respiratory irritation and potential damage to the respiratory tract. Due to its highly reactive nature, it can generate heat when dissolved in water, posing additional handling risks. Proper ventilation, controlled handling systems, and strict adherence to occupational safety standards are essential during storage and use.

Storage Guidelines:
  • - Store in a cool, dry, and well-ventilated area away from moisture and humidity
  • - Keep containers tightly sealed to prevent absorption of carbon dioxide and moisture
  • - Protect from exposure to air to avoid degradation and contamination
  • - Avoid contact with acids, metals, and incompatible substances
  • - Store different grades separately to maintain purity and prevent cross-contamination
Required PPE:
  • - Chemical-resistant gloves (PVC, neoprene, or nitrile)
  • - Safety goggles and face shields to prevent severe eye damage
  • - Protective clothing including aprons or suits for bulk handling
  • - Respiratory protection in environments with dust or mist formation
Handling Precautions:
  • - Avoid direct contact with skin, eyes, and clothing
  • - Use enclosed systems and automated handling wherever possible
  • - Add product slowly to water (never reverse) to control exothermic reactions
  • - Ensure proper ventilation and use of local exhaust systems
  • - Avoid generation and inhalation of dust or aerosols
  • - Maintain strict hygiene and emergency washing facilities in handling areas

Safety Data Sheets (SDS) are available upon request, providing detailed information on safe handling practices, exposure limits, and regulatory compliance.

Packaging & Transportation

Caustic soda is supplied in multiple physical forms including flakes, pearls, pellets, and liquid solutions, with packaging tailored to ensure safety, stability, and ease of handling across industrial applications. Packaging systems are designed to prevent moisture absorption, contamination, and ensure compatibility with corrosive properties.

Packaging Options:
  • HDPE Bags / Laminated Bags: Typically 25 kg bags for flakes, pearls, and solid forms
  • FIBC / Jumbo Bags: 500–1500 kg bulk packaging for large industrial consumption
  • Drums: HDPE or MS drums for smaller volume solid or semi-solid supply
  • Liquid Form: Supplied as caustic lye (30–50%) in tankers, drums, or IBC tanks
  • Pearl & Flake Grades: Moisture-resistant packaging to maintain product integrity
  • Low-Iron / Specialty Grades: Controlled packaging to preserve purity standards
  • Customized Packaging: Available based on export and application-specific requirements
Palletization & Export Packing:
  • - Bags palletized on wooden or plastic pallets (ISPM-15 compliant)
  • - Typically 40–50 bags per pallet depending on configuration
  • - Stretch-wrapped and securely strapped to prevent shifting
  • - Use of moisture barriers and liners for extended storage
  • - Proper labeling with grade, batch number, and hazard warnings
Container Loading & Handling:
  • - Loaded into 20 ft / 40 ft containers as per global safety standards
  • - Even weight distribution to maintain transport stability
  • - Use of corrosion-resistant handling equipment where required
  • - Pre-dispatch inspection ensures packaging and safety compliance
Transportation & Logistics:
  • - Classified as hazardous (corrosive) material under transport regulations
  • - Suitable for road, rail, and sea freight with proper handling protocols
  • - Requires compliance with hazardous material transportation guidelines
  • - Documentation support including COA, SDS, and regulatory declarations
  • - Full traceability maintained for quality and safety assurance
